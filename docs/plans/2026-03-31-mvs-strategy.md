# Minimum Viable Segment (MVS) Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Status:** Active

## The Problem: "For Everyone = For No One"

Our landing page currently optimizes for **visitors and consumers** ("Know your farmer. Visit the land.") while the product's core value — the AI-powered permaculture design tool — is buried. The people who will use the product daily (farmers/designers) get a single invisible line at the bottom of the page.

This means our highest-value users never see themselves in our marketing, while our lowest-engagement users (casual browsers) are front and center.

## Minimum Viable Segment: First-Time Permaculture Designers (0.5–5 acres)

### Who They Are
- Small landowners (backyard to small acreage) planning their first food forest or permaculture design
- Age 28–55, often new homesteaders, urban-to-rural transitioners, or curious suburban homeowners
- Have land, have intent, but lack expertise and confidence to start
- Budget-conscious — can't afford $2,000+ permaculture consultations

### 4 Us Evaluation

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **Unworkable** | High | Without a design plan, they plant randomly, waste money on plants that die, miss companion planting opportunities, and lose entire growing seasons. Poor placement = years of wasted growth. |
| **Unavoidable** | High | If you own land and want to grow food, you MUST decide what goes where. There is no way around spatial planning. |
| **Urgent** | High | Planting seasons are time-bound. Miss your spring window = lose an entire year of growth. Every month of indecision costs real food production. |
| **Underserved** | High | Current options: (1) Pen and paper — no AI, no species data, no growth simulation. (2) Generic garden planners — not permaculture-aware, no zones/guilds/layers. (3) Hire a consultant — $1,500–$5,000 for a single design. (4) Tend.com — limited mapping, no AI vision analysis. |

### Vitamin vs. Painkiller Analysis

**Current positioning (Vitamin):** "Explore farms, browse tours, shop from growers" — nice to have, no urgency, no pain being solved.

**Target positioning (Painkiller):** "You have land. You need a plan. Our AI designs your permaculture layout in minutes, not months." — urgent, specific, solves a real pain.

### The Pain We Solve
1. **Analysis paralysis**: Hundreds of species, dozens of companion combinations, 8 forest layers, 6+ zones — overwhelming for beginners
2. **Expensive expertise**: Professional permaculture design consultations cost $1,500–$5,000
3. **Seasonal urgency**: Wrong timing = lost year of growth
4. **Spatial complexity**: What goes where, which direction, which neighbors — requires expertise most people don't have

### Our 3D Breakthrough

| Dimension | How We Deliver |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Disruptive** | AI vision analysis of satellite imagery replaces expensive in-person consultations. Upload/screenshot your land → get a permaculture design. |
| **Discontinuous** | Previously impossible: real-time growth simulation showing what your food forest looks like in 1, 5, 10 years. No other tool does this. |
| **Defensible** | Native species database + regional knowledge network + community-shared designs create a data moat that grows with every user. |

### Gain-Pain Ratio (Targeting 10x)

**Gain:**
- Professional-quality permaculture design in minutes (vs. weeks of research or $2k+ consultation)
- AI that understands your specific land, climate zone, and soil
- Growth simulation so you can SEE the future before you plant
- Native species recommendations that actually work in your region

**Adoption Pain (what we must minimize):**
- Learning a new tool (mitigate: guided onboarding wizard)
- Trusting AI recommendations (mitigate: cite permaculture principles, show reasoning)
- Drawing on a map (mitigate: simple polygon tools, snap-to-grid)
- No immediate physical result (mitigate: exportable planting plans, seasonal task lists)

## Landing Page Implications

1. **Hero must speak to farmers first** — visitors are a secondary audience
2. **Show the design tool in action** — the map editor IS the product
3. **Lead with the pain** — "You have land. Now what?" is more compelling than "Know your farmer"
4. **Add a prominent grower section** with specific pain points and solutions
5. **CTAs should drive to farm creation**, not gallery browsing
